Free Web Editors

An HTML editor is a computer program for editing HTML. Although the HTML markup of a web page can be written with any text editor, specialized HTML editors can offer convenience and added functionality. Many HTML editors handle not only HTML, but also related technologies such as CSS, and JavaScript. There are hundreds of editors available on the web, some good examples include:

  • Adobe Dreamweaver is one of the most well known WYSIWYG editors. A WYSIWYG, or "what you see is what you get" editor, allows a developer to see what the end result will look like while the document is still being created. $19.99/month.
  • Notepad++ (Windows)
  • TextWrangler (Mac)
  • KompoZer (ideal for people without extensive technical computer skills)
  • Weebly (WYSIWYG editor, no technical experience needed)
  • Bluegriffon (Another WYSIWYG editor)
  • Komodo Edit (provides the basic functionality you need for programming)

Free Web Hosts

Web hosts provide the technologies and services needed for the website or webpage to be viewed on the Internet. Websites are hosted, or stored, on special computers called servers.

When Internet users want to view your website, all they need to do is type your website address into their browser. Their computer will then connect to the hosting server and your webpages will be delivered to the veiwer through the browser.

Most hosting companies require that you own your domain name in order to host with them. If you do not have a domain name, the hosting companies will help you purchase one.

  • ehost.com
  • Web Hosting Hub
  • 000webhost
  • AwardSpace.com

Types of Web Hosts

Free Web Hosting: Most free hosting services are funded by advertising that appears on your site, so free web hosting is generally best for personal, rather than business, websites

Paid Hosting: With paid hosting, you pay a fee for space and services on a web hosting provider's server. Monthly fees can range from a few dollars to several hundred dollars.

Domain Hosting: A good option for small businesses. Domain hosting allows you to host your site anywhere you like: wether it be on a free hosting service, or even your own server. You buy a domain name and have the provider forward all requests for that domain to your actual web location. This is often less expensive than buying both the domain and the hosting service, and it allows businesses to customize their URLs.
